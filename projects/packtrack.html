July 2025

Visual Design Direction

The Flipper Zero display is 128×64 pixels of monochrome — every pixel matters. The visual language for PackTrack is built on Flipper-native conventions so the app feels at home on the device, not like a transplant from a phone.

Native Flipper grammar

List-driven menus, dpad navigation, single-line headers — match the system apps so users don't relearn anything.

Status word over status code

"Out for delivery" reads better than "DEPARTED 04A." Carrier codes get translated to plain language before they hit the screen.

Glyphs for carriers

Tiny carrier logos (USPS, UPS, FedEx) render in monochrome bitmap so users identify packages at a glance.

Loading is honest

HTTP calls take time on a Flipper — a clear "fetching…" line with a tiny spinner replaces silent waits, so users never think the device froze.

June 2025

Target Users

PackTrack is for the slice of Flipper Zero owners who want their device to do real, daily work — not just RF tricks. Two patterns drive the design.

FT

The Flipper Tinkerer

Treats the Flipper as a daily tool, not a toy

Goals

  • Use the Flipper for real utility, not gimmicks
  • Quick info checks without unlocking a phone
  • Apps that respect Flipper conventions

Pain Points

  • Most third-party apps feel half-baked
  • Network-dependent apps rarely work reliably
FB

The Frequent Buyer

Has multiple packages in flight at any time

Goals

  • One screen, all packages, current status
  • Skip carrier websites that all look different
  • Quick "is it close?" answer

Pain Points

  • Three carriers means three apps
  • Notification spam without a unified view

June 2025

Problem

Traditional package tracking requires a smartphone or computer to constantly go online to check shipping provider sites for delivery information, creating friction when users just want a quick status glance.

The challenge was bringing this utility to an embedded device with limited connectivity and display capabilities.
