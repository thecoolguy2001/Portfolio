Visual Design Direction

Apparel e-commerce competes on confidence — a customer has to trust that the brand is real before they hand over money for a piece they can't try on. The visual language was built around that trust.

Photography is the brand

Editorial-style product imagery dominates the surface. The site is a frame for the lookbook, not the other way around.

One brand voice everywhere

Type, color, and tone match across product, cart, email, and confirmation pages. No Shopify-default checkout aesthetics breaking the spell.

Trust signals up front

Free returns, sizing guides, customer photos. Surfaced where the buying decision happens — not buried in a footer.

Mobile-first sizing

Most checkouts happen on phones. Touch targets, sticky add-to-cart, and a sizing drawer were designed for the small screen first.

Target Shoppers

Two shopper patterns shaped the site — the social-led discovery shopper and the returning brand loyalist. Both had to land on a site that felt premium.

SD

The Social Discoverer

Saw the brand on Instagram or TikTok

Goals

  • Confirm the brand is real and shippable
  • Find the piece they saw, see fit photos
  • Buy without creating an account

Pain Points

  • Fly-by-night brands lose trust fast
  • Default Shopify sites all look the same
RC

The Returning Customer

Already owns a piece, watching for the next drop

Goals

  • See what's new, fast
  • Re-buy in the same size confidently
  • Stay in the loyalty / email loop

Pain Points

  • Sites that don't remember last size
  • Drop announcements lost in inbox noise
