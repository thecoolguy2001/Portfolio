March 2024

Visual Design Direction

Vosyn is an AI startup — the website had to read as serious technology while still feeling welcoming to non-technical visitors and prospective employees. The visual language was tuned for both audiences.

Tech-confident, not tech-bro

Restrained color palette, generous spacing, modern sans typography. The site signals seriousness without falling into the dark-mode-with-gradients tech aesthetic.

CTA hierarchy

One primary CTA per page (waitlist), one secondary (careers). Eliminated competing buttons that diluted intent.

Careers as a real surface

The careers page became a design priority — culture imagery, role descriptions, and clear application paths. Recruiting was a strategic outcome, not an afterthought.

Accessibility as a feature

The accessibility page was rebuilt to genuinely meet standards, not just claim them. Visible commitment to a/11y reads as a trust signal across the brand.

Core Visitor Flow

The site has two real audiences — prospects who might join the waitlist, and candidates considering Vosyn as an employer. Both flows had to feel deliberate.

02

February 2024

Target Visitors

The redesign served two distinct audiences. Both had to feel equally important from the homepage forward.

EP

The Early Prospect

Curious about the product, evaluating whether to join the waitlist

Goals

  • Understand what Vosyn does in under a minute
  • See enough to decide if the waitlist is worth it
  • Frictionless signup once they decide

Pain Points

  • AI startup sites often overpromise vaguely
  • Noisy CTAs distract from the core ask
PC

The Prospective Candidate

Looking at the company as a place to work

Goals

  • Get a real read on the company culture
  • See open roles and what's expected
  • Understand how to apply

Pain Points

  • Most startup careers pages are boilerplate
  • Hard to tell what the team actually values
