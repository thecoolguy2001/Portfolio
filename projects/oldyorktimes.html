February 2025

Visual Design Direction

Old York Times had to evoke 1930s newspaper without becoming a costume. The visual language pulls genuine details — column grid, hairline rules, drop caps — and pairs them with modern reading affordances.

Period typography

Display serifs for headlines, transitional serifs for body. Italic small caps for bylines. The type does most of the period-setting work.

Grid like a press sheet

Multi-column layout with narrow gutters and hairline rules. Mimics letterpress structure without sacrificing screen readability.

Cream paper, ink black

Off-white background reduces glare and reads as paper. Pure black for text — period-correct and high contrast.

Modern only where it helps

Search, navigation, sticky headers — modern conveniences hide inside vintage chrome so the reading experience stays in character.

September 2024

Target Readers

Two reader patterns shaped the site — readers who come for the aesthetic, and readers who come for the writing. Both had to feel served.

DR

The Design Reader

Comes for the aesthetic, stays for a story

Goals

  • An online publication that feels considered
  • A break from doomscroll-shaped news feeds
  • Something visually worth sharing

Pain Points

  • Modern news sites all look the same
  • Aesthetic-first sites usually lack substance
SF

The Short-Fiction Fan

Reads pulp, lit mags, and Substack short stories

Goals

  • Discover original short fiction in a polished home
  • Reading layout that respects the writing
  • An archive worth flipping back through

Pain Points

  • Lit-mag sites are often barebones HTML
  • Modern publishing platforms strip period flavor

August 2024

The Problem

Looking at current news and article sites, they seem to have become less expressive and creative for users, with some even adding unwanted complexity to their design.

The challenge was to craft a digital experience that captures the essence of old pulp fiction and 1930s news while meeting today's standards for usability.

July 2024

Working with Writers & Artists

Old York Times is a publication, not a static template — the design only works if it gives writers and visual contributors a place to do good work. Collaboration shaped the system from the start.

Collaboration

Writers

Worked with short-fiction writers to shape the article template — column widths, drop-cap behavior, and pull-quote handling were tuned to how they actually wrote.

Collaboration

Artist

Partnered with an illustrator on cover art and section glyphs. The visual identity of each edition came from those pieces — the design system was built around them.

Editorial Loop

Edition Cadence

Issues ship as discrete editions, not a rolling feed. The cadence forced clear handoff points between writing, art, and layout — and gave the publication a real publishing rhythm.
