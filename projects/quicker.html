Results & Reception

QuickER demonstrated a viable solution for reducing ER wait times and improving the patient intake experience. The prototype received positive feedback for its intuitive interface and practical approach to a common healthcare pain point.

Through this project, I learned the importance of user-centered design and iterative development. The experience taught me how to effectively gather and incorporate user feedback to create a product that meets the needs of its users.

June 2023

Design Iterations

The app evolved through multiple iterations focused on simplifying the check-in process.

v3.0

Streamlined Flow

Reduced check-in steps from 8 to 4, added progress indicators, and implemented auto-save for partial completions.

v2.0

Queue Visualization

Added real-time queue position display, estimated wait times, and push notifications for status updates.

v1.0

Core Check-in

Basic remote check-in functionality with patient information forms and hospital selection.

May 2023

The Solution

A digital platform for patients to check in to the ER remotely, securing their place in the queue before arriving.

Remote Check-in

Complete intake forms from anywhere, eliminating redundant paperwork at the hospital.

Real-time Updates

Track queue position and receive notifications when it's time to head to the ER.

Efficient Intake

Seamless handoff to hospital systems reduces processing time and improves patient flow.

May 2023

The Problem

Patients with non-life-threatening conditions often face long wait times and a cumbersome intake process in emergency rooms.

This results in patient dissatisfaction and inefficiencies for healthcare providers who must manage overcrowded waiting areas.

May 2023

Project Started

The development of QuickER aimed to streamline the emergency room (ER) experience for patients and healthcare providers.

The project focused on reducing wait times, improving patient satisfaction, and optimizing the intake process in hospitals through a user-friendly mobile application.
