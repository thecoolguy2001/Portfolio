Visual Design Direction

Healthcare apps tend to feel cold, anxious, or both. The visual language for QuickER had to feel calm and clear — patients are often using the app at their lowest, not their best.

Calm color, no alarm

Soft blues and warm neutrals. Red is reserved for urgent triage prompts only — overuse would normalize the alert.

Generous touch targets

Buttons sized for shaky, anxious thumbs. Form fields auto-advance when valid so users keep momentum.

Plain language, no jargon

"Where does it hurt?" beats "Identify presenting symptom location." The app talks like a nurse, not a database.

Progress visible at all times

A persistent step indicator tells the user how far they are. Anxious users need to know when this ends.

The Solution

A digital platform for patients to check in to the ER remotely, securing their place in the queue before arriving.

Remote Check-in

Complete intake forms from anywhere, eliminating redundant paperwork at the hospital.

Real-time Updates

Track queue position and receive notifications when it's time to head to the ER.

Efficient Intake

Seamless handoff to hospital systems reduces processing time and improves patient flow.

May 2023

The Problem

Patients with non-life-threatening conditions often face long wait times and a cumbersome intake process in emergency rooms.

This results in patient dissatisfaction and inefficiencies for healthcare providers who must manage overcrowded waiting areas.

May 2023

Target Patients

Two patient patterns shaped the design — non-life-threatening conditions where the wait dominates the experience, and parents managing a child's care.

NU

The Non-Urgent Patient

Knows it's not life-threatening but needs to be seen

Goals

  • Skip the in-person waiting room
  • Know when to head over
  • Avoid filling out paperwork twice

Pain Points

  • Hours of in-person waiting feels punishing
  • Repetitive intake forms at every step
CG

The Caregiver

Bringing a child or family member to the ER

Goals

  • Manage logistics while caring for someone
  • Get clear answers about wait times
  • One source of truth on the phone

Pain Points

  • Calling the ER for status is brittle
  • Caregiving and logistics compete for attention
