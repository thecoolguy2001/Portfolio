October 2025

Visual Design Direction

A watch face is one glance away from being closed. Every visual choice was made for legibility at arm's length and instant comprehension of the next command.

One word, full screen

Each command word fills the watch face. No icons fighting for attention — the prompt is the screen.

Color = command

Bop, Twist, and Shake each get a distinct hue. Color reinforces the audio cue for players who lower the volume.

Animation as countdown

A radial timer drains around the screen edge — players feel the time pressure without reading a number.

Haptic-first feedback

Right and wrong have distinct haptic signatures. The wrist tells you the result before your eyes catch up.

Solution

Reflexo uses SwiftUI for fluid UI components, AVFoundation for crisp voice prompts, and WatchKit haptics for tactile feedback.

Voice Commands

Randomized Bop It, Twist It, and Shake It commands keep players on their toes.

Haptic Feedback

WatchKit haptics provide satisfying tactile responses for every action.

Real-time Animations

SwiftUI delivers smooth, responsive visual feedback during gameplay.

July 2025

Target Users

Reflex-o targets two play patterns common to watch users — micro-sessions waiting for something, and quick social challenges.

QL

The Queue Filler

Fills the wait at a coffee bar, an elevator, a transit stop

Goals

  • Something fun for under a minute
  • No phone, no friction
  • Resume right where they left off

Pain Points

  • Most watch apps are utility-only
  • Phone games are too much for a 45-second wait
SC

The Streak Chaser

Loves a personal best to beat and a friend to beat too

Goals

  • Climb their own high score
  • Compare scores with friends
  • Quick rounds that snowball into more rounds

Pain Points

  • Reflex games rarely fit on a watch
  • Lack of haptics makes most ports feel hollow

Problem

Smartwatch games often struggle with limited screen real estate, unreliable audio integration, and lackluster feedback loops.

The challenge was to create a game that felt fun, quick to pick up, and perfectly sized for the wrist.
