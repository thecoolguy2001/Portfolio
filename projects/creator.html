April 2024

Visual Design Direction

Modern and nostalgic is a tough combination — most attempts pick one and gesture at the other. The visual language committed to both: classic blog warmth in the type and texture, modern grid logic in the layout.

Editorial type pairing

Display serif for headlines, humanist sans for body. Reads like a classic blog without looking dated.

Warm neutrals

Cream paper, deep charcoal text, single warm accent. Avoids the cold tech-blog default and lets the writing breathe.

Modern responsive grid

Multi-column layout that gracefully reflows on mobile. The "blog feel" survives the small screen, where most reading happens.

Interactions, but quiet

Subtle hover and scroll animations keep the site feeling alive. Nothing flashy — the writing is the headline act.

Working with the Creator

The whole engagement was one month — that put a premium on tight collaboration loops. Discovery, review, and handoff had to be fast without losing fidelity.

A single working session up front to align on tone, references, and the creator's audience. Mood-board-driven so we could move past abstract adjectives.

Review

Handoff

Built on Webflow specifically so the creator could publish new posts on her own. The site has to be hers after launch, not mine.

April 2024

Target Readers

Two reader patterns shaped the site — long-time fans following the creator across platforms, and new readers landing from search.

RF

The Returning Fan

Already follows the creator on social, wants more depth

Goals

  • Read longer pieces than fit on social
  • Get notified when new posts drop
  • Catch up on the back catalog

Pain Points

  • Most creator sites are abandoned link trees
  • No reading mode that respects the work
SR

The Search Reader

Found a post via Google or a link

Goals

  • Read this post without obstruction
  • Decide quickly whether to read more
  • Find the next post if interested

Pain Points

  • Most blogs hit you with popups before the headline
  • Hard to tell who's behind a single post
