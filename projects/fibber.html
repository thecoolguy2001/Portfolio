October 2025

Visual Design Direction

Fibber walks a careful line — it has to feel theatrical enough to be fun, but not so clinical that users mistake it for a medical tool. Every visual decision sells the entertainment framing.

Heart as the hero

A pulsing heart graphic anchors the screen. The visual rhythm matches the live BPM — users see their heartbeat before they read a number.

Three-state verdict

Green, yellow, red for Truth, Uncertain, Lie. Color does the announcing — text is secondary confirmation.

Game-show typography

Bold display weight on results. Reads more like a quiz reveal than a hospital readout.

Honesty in the copy

Onboarding flat-out tells users this is for fun. No fake authority — entertainment is the whole point.

July 2025

Target Users

Fibber is aimed at people who use the watch socially — at parties, with friends, at the dinner table. Two patterns drive the design.

PG

The Party Game Host

Pulls out the watch to start a moment with friends

Goals

  • Get a group laughing fast
  • Pass the watch around without setup
  • A clear, dramatic verdict everyone can see

Pain Points

  • Most party games need a phone or a board
  • Watch apps rarely turn into shared moments
CP

The Curious Personal-Health User

Already tracks their HR, wants to play with it

Goals

  • See heart-rate variability in a fun frame
  • Notice their own stress reactions
  • Use it as conversation, not diagnosis

Pain Points

  • Health apps make biometric data feel clinical
  • No casual interface for HR-based curiosity

July 2025

Problem

Users are intrigued by polygraphs but lack accessible, personal tools to explore biometric feedback.

The challenge was making biometric data feel emotional and entertaining rather than clinical.
