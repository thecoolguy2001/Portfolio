November 2023

Visual Design Direction

The interface had to feel lightweight and modern — a dating-adjacent product that didn't look like a dating app. The visual language pulls from short-form video culture, not stock romance imagery.

Content over chrome

The matched person's content sits at the center of every card. Profile fields are secondary — what they watch is the introduction.

Vertical rhythm

Layouts and motion echo the vertical-feed muscle memory users already have from TikTok — no learning curve.

Honest privacy framing

"What we share, what we don't" written plainly into the onboarding screens. Trust is the product's foundation.

Quiet brand

One soft accent color, generous spacing, no skeuomorphic hearts or sparkles. Feels like a utility, not a casino.

August 2023

Target Users

Two user types informed every screen — both fed up with bio-based matching, both already living in the algorithmic feed.

TN

The TikTok-Native

Spends hours on the FYP — that feed is more "them" than any bio

Goals

  • Match on humor, taste, and niche interests
  • Skip the curated photo / canned-prompt grind
  • Find someone who would send the same videos

Pain Points

  • Dating app bios feel performative and shallow
  • Mismatched senses of humor surface on date one
FS

The Friendship Seeker

Looking for friends with overlapping niches, not necessarily romance

Goals

  • Connect with people who watch what they watch
  • Skip the dating-coded photo grids
  • Low-stakes way to message someone first

Pain Points

  • Most matching apps assume romantic intent
  • Friend-finding apps are sparse and awkward

August 2023

The Problem

The challenge was to develop an app that could effectively match users based on their TikTok activity, ensuring a seamless and enjoyable experience.

Traditional matching apps rely on self-reported interests, which can be inaccurate. Content consumption patterns provide a more authentic view of someone's personality.
