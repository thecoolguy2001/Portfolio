July 2024

Xcode Build & Project Completion

After the Flutter build, the next month was spent rebuilding the same app in Xcode with Swift — a parallel implementation to compare the two stacks on a real product, not a tutorial. Once the Xcode version reached a working test build, PeerPair was left in testing mode and not pursued further. The point of the project was the experiment itself: to see what it took to build an app like this, end to end, in two different toolchains.

Two stacks, same app

Building the same product twice — once in Flutter, once in Xcode/Swift — surfaced the real differences between cross-platform and native, in a way reading docs never would.

Testing mode, not shipping mode

The goal was learning, not launching. Both builds reached a testable state and stopped there — the experiment had paid off.

Knowledge banked

The patterns from this build — TikTok API integration, content-based matching UI, swipe-stack mechanics — became reusable knowledge for later iOS / mobile work.

June 2024

Flutter Build

Wrapped up the Flutter implementation of PeerPair — one codebase running on both iOS and Android, with the TikTok API integration, profile setup, match card stack, and basic chat all working end to end. Flutter let the design ideas land on a real device fast; the cross-platform reach was the right fit for an experiment that was meant to be tested, not shipped.

Cross-platform reach

One Flutter codebase ran on both iOS and Android test devices — important for a social app where any tester might be on either side.

API plumbing

TikTok content endpoints integrated for the matching feed. Auth, scope handling, and rate-limit backoff all wired up against real responses.

Match-card mechanics

The swipe-stack with inline TikTok preview was the key surface — most of the build time went into making it feel right under thumb.

November 2023

Visual Design Direction

The interface had to feel lightweight and modern — a dating-adjacent product that didn't look like a dating app. The visual language pulls from short-form video culture, not stock romance imagery.

Content over chrome

The matched person's content sits at the center of every card. Profile fields are secondary — what they watch is the introduction.

Vertical rhythm

Layouts and motion echo the vertical-feed muscle memory users already have from TikTok — no learning curve.

Honest privacy framing

"What we share, what we don't" written plainly into the onboarding screens. Trust is the product's foundation.

Quiet brand

One soft accent color, generous spacing, no skeuomorphic hearts or sparkles. Feels like a utility, not a casino.

October 2023

The Solution

A mobile app that utilizes TikTok's API to gather user data and creates a matching algorithm to connect users with similar interests.

Content-Based Matching

Matches users based on the TikTok content they engage with, ensuring shared interests.

Fun Experience

Gamified interface makes discovering new connections enjoyable and engaging.

Privacy First

Users control what data is shared and can disconnect their TikTok at any time.

August 2023

Target Users

Two user types informed every screen — both fed up with bio-based matching, both already living in the algorithmic feed.

TN

The TikTok-Native

Spends hours on the FYP — that feed is more "them" than any bio

Goals

  • Match on humor, taste, and niche interests
  • Skip the curated photo / canned-prompt grind
  • Find someone who would send the same videos

Pain Points

  • Dating app bios feel performative and shallow
  • Mismatched senses of humor surface on date one
FS

The Friendship Seeker

Looking for friends with overlapping niches, not necessarily romance

Goals

  • Connect with people who watch what they watch
  • Skip the dating-coded photo grids
  • Low-stakes way to message someone first

Pain Points

  • Most matching apps assume romantic intent
  • Friend-finding apps are sparse and awkward

August 2023

The Problem

The challenge was to develop an app that could effectively match users based on their TikTok activity, ensuring a seamless and enjoyable experience.

Traditional matching apps rely on self-reported interests, which can be inaccurate. Content consumption patterns provide a more authentic view of someone's personality.

July 2023

Project Started

The development of the PeerPair app aimed to create a unique platform that matches users based on their TikTok activity and preferences.

The project focused on leveraging user-generated content to facilitate connections and provide a fun and engaging user experience.

Project Complete

PeerPair was built on both Flutter and Xcode and left in testing mode. The project ended in July 2024 as an experiment in building an app of this kind across two toolchains.
