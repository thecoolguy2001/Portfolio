December 2023

Visual Design Direction

OURS is a production studio — its work is its proof. The redesign was built to put the work front-and-center, with the brand recede so the reels could speak.

Reel-first layout

Big video and image hero, clean modular grid for project tiles. Visitors should see real production work within seconds.

Restrained brand

One typography pair, neutral palette, single accent. The brand is the wrapper, not the show.

Editable on the studio side

Built so the non-technical studio team could update reels and case studies without engineering. The site has to live without me after handoff.

Cohesive across studio properties

Visual system extended to related sites and email templates so the studio felt like one brand across surfaces.

The Problem

OURS Studio had an outdated online presence that lacked modern design elements and a cohesive aesthetic.

The primary issues included low-quality images, lack of modern features, and inconsistent design that didn't reflect the quality of their production work.

November 2023

Working with the Studio Team

This was an internship with a working production studio. The team was less tech-oriented, which meant a lot of the value I added was in translating between design intent and what was buildable in WordPress without ongoing engineering support.

November 2023

Target Visitors

The site had to win two distinct audiences within seconds of arrival — clients who could hire the studio, and peers in the industry whose word-of-mouth fuels future work.

PC

The Prospective Client

Brand or agency considering the studio for a project

Goals

  • See the level of work, fast
  • Understand what the studio specializes in
  • Get to "let's talk" without friction

Pain Points

  • Studio sites that hide the reel behind menus
  • Out-of-date reels signal a slow studio
IP

The Industry Peer

Director, DP, or post collaborator browsing the field

Goals

  • See who shot what, with whom
  • Find the studio's full credit list quickly
  • Pull stills or BTS for reference

Pain Points

  • Most studio sites bury credits
  • No clear path to the people behind the work
