July 2025

Visual Design Direction

Most of the experience is audio and silence — the screen is the smallest part of Roulette. The visual language was tuned to add to suspense without distracting from it.

Big icon, no chrome

One central glyph dominates the screen — a chamber, a trigger. Negative space carries the tension.

Audio is the lead

The buzzer carries the drama; the screen reacts. Visual flashes punctuate sound, never compete with it.

Result, then silence

The outcome screen shows for a beat, then returns to start. No celebration loops — the next round can land immediately.

Theatrical typography

"CLICK" or "BANG" rendered in big bold pixel type. The verdict is the headline.

July 2025

Solution

Roulette is a minimal game where each player pulls the trigger by pressing the center button. A sound plays, tension builds, and either silence (survival) or a dramatic buzzer (you're out!) delivers the outcome.

Simple Controls

One button to play. Press the center button to pull the trigger.

Audio Tension

Sound builds suspense before revealing the outcome.

Dramatic Feedback

Clear audio distinction between survival and elimination.

Core User Flow

June 2025

Target Users

Roulette lives at parties and hangouts — places where the Flipper Zero gets passed around like a card. Two audiences shaped the design.

PH

The Party Host

Pulls the Flipper out as a conversation starter

Goals

  • One-button game everyone gets immediately
  • High-stakes feeling, low actual stakes
  • Quick rounds that pass around a circle

Pain Points

  • Most digital party games need a phone per player
  • Setup time kills the moment
FE

The Flipper Enthusiast

Wants apps that show the device's personality

Goals

  • Apps that feel made for the Flipper, not ported to it
  • Use the buzzer and screen for something memorable
  • Show off what the device can do

Pain Points

  • Most third-party apps are clones of phone apps
  • Few games take advantage of the buzzer

June 2025

Problem

The Flipper Zero is widely known for its tools and hacking modules, but it lacks playful, high-stakes games that utilize the device's buzzer, screen, and input in a theatrical way.

The challenge was creating entertainment value through tension and pacing alone.
