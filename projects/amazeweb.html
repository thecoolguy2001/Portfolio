2024

Amaze Web Storefront — The Service Gets a Front Door

For four years the agency had run on inquiry alone — clients found me through word-of-mouth, referrals, and direct asks, and the engagement happened over email. There was no public storefront. In 2024 I built one: a real site at amazeweb.shop that gave the service a single place to send prospects to, browse case studies, see service tiers, and submit a structured inquiry that flowed straight into the engagement process.

Built with the client stack

The storefront uses the same tools I deploy for clients (Webflow + Framer with a custom layer) — eat what you cook. The agency's own site is part of the portfolio.

Transparent pricing tiers

Service tiers and "what's included" are listed publicly. Small businesses know within thirty seconds whether a tier fits their budget — no quote dance to find out.

Live case studies

Each major client engagement gets a real case-study page — the site, the brief, the role, the deliverables. Prospects can read past work without an intro call.

Inquiry form → engagement flow

The form captures what, when, and budget range, then routes directly into the discovery / scope / design / build / handoff loop documented in the 2020 entry.

Designed for an overhaul

The 2024 site was built modular so it could grow with the service. In 2026 it's getting that overhaul — Framer templates for sale, AI-agent integration packages, and the new email design service all surface on the storefront so visitors land on the right path for what they actually need.

2024

Framer Joins the Toolkit

Added Framer to the stack for client sites. Its blend of clean design control, native motion, and tight CMS handling fit creative-leaning clients who needed visual polish without giving up the ability to edit content themselves. Most new client sites this year shipped on Framer.

Right tool, right client

Framer doesn't replace Webflow or WordPress on the menu — it joins them. The right platform for a client depends on the client's content rhythm, technical comfort, and budget.

Motion as default, not feature

Framer makes motion cheap to design well. Clients get sites that move with intent, not just sites that scroll.

2022

Process & Client Practice

By the second full year of taking clients, the engagement loop had earned the right to be standardized — and the honest realities of working with people had started teaching their own lessons. Both the artifacts and the friction matter. A case study without the friction is a sales pitch.

Process artifacts on every engagement:

Wireframes first, visuals second

Every project starts low-fi: page-level wireframes, content blocks, IA tree, user flows. The visual design layer goes on once the structure is locked. Saved cycles of rework downstream.

Two-round design reviews

Wireframes get one review pass; visual design gets another. Locking the structure before the polish keeps client feedback focused on the right layer at the right time.

Living documentation

Every site ships with a CMS guide tailored to the client's literacy level — short-loom walkthroughs plus written steps — so they can edit content without me after handoff.

Async Loom check-ins

Most design walkthroughs are recorded video, not live calls. Clients review on their schedule and reply with timestamps. Faster, kinder, and far better documented than meetings.

Tool decisions tied to client reality

"Webflow or WordPress or Framer?" gets answered by the content rhythm a client can realistically maintain a year out — not what they ask for on day one. The right platform is whichever one stays usable after I'm gone.

Usability checks before launch

A short pre-launch test pass — five real users tap through the site on phone and desktop. Catches broken nav, mystery-meat icons, and blocking copy issues before the client sees them.

Client realities — the part not in the case study slides:

Scope creep

The perennial. Solved with precise scopes (what's in, what's explicitly out) and a clear "phase 2" pricing path for wishlist items — instead of saying yes to everything inside the original budget.

Conflicting stakeholders inside small teams

When a founder and co-founder disagreed on direction, the right move was almost never to design both options — it was to run one shared review session and let them resolve it together while I held the structure.

Communication mismatches

Some clients work in real-time chats, others over weekly emails. Setting the cadence in the discovery call — not assuming — saved a lot of friction. The engagement should fit the client's rhythm, not mine.

Knowing when to walk

A small number of engagements ended early when scope, budget, or expectations weren't going to align. Walking away cleanly — refunding deposits, returning files, leaving a polite door open — is a real skill, and one of the harder ones to learn.

2021

Adopted Webflow

Started building client sites in Webflow in 2021. The visual builder, real CMS, and clean code export made it the right fit for clients who needed both design polish and editable content — without the maintenance overhead of a hand-coded build. From this point on, most new clients had a "Webflow vs. WordPress" decision baked into the discovery call.

Pain Points

  • Free templates look generic and unprofessional

  • Brand-matched email design and flows
  • Open the door to AI assistant integrations

Pain Points

  • Stitching together multiple platforms is brittle

2020

The Problem

Small businesses, indie brands, and creators wanted a real, polished web presence — but couldn't afford traditional agency rates and didn't trust generic free templates to represent them. Free options looked free. Agencies wanted five figures and three months. There was a gap in the middle: agency-quality work at fixed, transparent prices, with a clear engagement flow.

2020

First Client Site — Amaze Web Begins

The first paid client site shipped in 2020, built on WordPress. That first project became the template — same five-step engagement, same emphasis on letting the client edit their own content after launch — and Amaze Web has been taking clients through that same flow ever since.
