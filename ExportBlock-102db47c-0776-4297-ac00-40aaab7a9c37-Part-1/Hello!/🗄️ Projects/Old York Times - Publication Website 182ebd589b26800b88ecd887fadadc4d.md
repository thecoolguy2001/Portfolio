# Summary

The development of the Old York Times website was built to evoke the timeless charm of pulp fiction and classic journalism, the Old York Times website redefines news delivery for modern audiences. Designed to engage users with both current news and fictional stories, the site blends a nostalgic aesthetic with modern interactive features giving users truly a unique experience. 

# Problem

Looking a current news and article sites, they seem to have become less expressive and creative for users, with some even added unwanted complexity to its site design. The challenge was to craft a digital experience that captures the essence of old pulp fiction and 1930s news while meeting today‚Äôs standards for usability and engagement. The site needed to deliver real-time news alongside imaginative fictional storytelling in a way that was both authentic to its vintage roots and appealing to modern audiences.

The solution involved developing the site as a unique platform that merges the timeless look of vintage newspapers with contemporary digital design. The initial iteration focused on establishing the core vintage aesthetic. This phase involved creating a layout inspired by 1930s newspapers, complete with classic typography and a structured, grid-based format that supported both news and fictional content.

### - Iteration 2.

![Old York Times (5).png](Old%20York%20Times%20-%20Publication%20Website/Old_York_Times_(5).png)

In the second iteration, modern interactive elements were incorporated. Enhancements included dynamic content sections, interactive navigation, and responsive design features that made the site accessible on all devices while preserving its retro charm. This also included ensuring the content and artwork were done correctly to fit the aesthetic and styling. 

### - Iteration 3.
